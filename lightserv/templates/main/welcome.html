Welcome to the Brain Registration and Histology Core Facility Portal at the Princeton Neuroscience Institute

What does this Core Facility do?

The Brain Registration and Histology Core Facility is one of the five cores of BRAIN CoGS. In this core, we perform whole-brain clearing and light-sheet imaging for a variety of tissue types upon request. See our gallery for example tissue types and applications. The Core Facility currently supports the following clearing protocols: iDISCO+ (adapted from Renier et al. 2016) | iDISCO for nonoxidizable fluorophores | iDISCO for nonoxidizable fluorophores (for rats) | uDISCO (adapted from Pan et al. 2017) | uDISCO (for rats). If you are interested in using immunostaining, please read this . Experimental protocols are also allowed, but please communicate with us before submitting a request if you plan to try a protocol not in the above list. Note on antibody labeling. How to use this portal?

If you are interested in using the Core Facility's services, please fill out a new request form. A "request" is a collection of tubes from a single species that you would like cleared, imaged and processed. In the form, you will be asked to provide the details of your request, such as how many tubes you have and the clearing and imaging parameters to use for each tube. These parameters can be different for different tubes within a single request, but in general it is a good idea to group tubes that are in some way related in a single request. For example, a single request could be brains from 10 mouse subjects coming from multiple experimental groups that underwent the same behavioral task. You may request up to 50 tubes in a single request. If this is your first time using the core facility, please read these instructions regarding where and when to physically hand off your samples: Pre-handoff instructions.

What data products do we provide?

The default data products are raw light sheet images at each image resolution and wavelength specified when you filled out the request form. You can also elect to use our automated processing pipeline which combines the raw light sheet images ("blending"), stitches multiple tiles together if present, then downsamples and registers the images to a reference atlas, provided that an atlas is available for the species in your request. The Core Facility currently supports two versions of the Allen Mouse Brain atlas (pre and post-2017) as well as an in-house light sheet atlas (the "Princeton Mouse Atlas"; Pisano et al., in prep.) that provides superior registration accuracy and precision for light sheet data.

At your Requests page, you will see an overview and the status of all of your ongoing and completed requests. The parameters of your clearing and imaging data are automatically stored in a Datajoint database upon your completion of the new request form for access external to this site. This jupyter notebook explains how to access your light sheet data and metadata via Datajoint/Python. Visualizations of your data at various stages in the pipeline are also available. See the FAQ or contact us for more details.

How to contact us?

If you cannot find the answer to your question in the FAQ, please email us at lightservhelper@gmail.com with any questions about new, ongoing or previous requests, or to simply provide feedback on your experience with this portal or the Core Facility.
