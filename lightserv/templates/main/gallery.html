Transsynaptic viral tracing

The original use case of the Core Facility. Pisano et al. (in prep.) used light sheet imaging to map anatomical pathways between the cerebellum and sensorimotor, associative, and modulatory regions of the forebrain. Shown above is a three dimensional visualization of cell centers (light green) that were traced using the anterograde transsynaptic tracer herpes simplex virus type 1 (H129) and the retrograde tracer pseudorabies virus (Bartha). Also shown are cerebellar subregions from the Princeton Mouse Brain Atlas (PMA), a modified version of the Allen Mouse Brain Atlas (AMBA). The PMA extends the AMBA to include the posterior cerebellum, and it improves registration accuracy for light sheet images.

Tech specs:
Species: Mouse
Clearing protocol: iDISCO+ (with immunostaining)
Imaging resolution: 4x
Imaging modes: Registration (488 nm), Cell detection (647 nm)
Cell detection method: https://github.com/PrincetonUniversity/3dunet
